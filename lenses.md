# Remix Lens Palette

Creative raw material for the **Scout**. Buckets define how FAR from the idea to reach. Seeds are spurs, **not a cage** — pick the spiciest *specific* domain for the idea at hand, and always add wildcards beyond these lists.

A domain is what you steal FROM. A technique is how you bend the idea WITH it. The Scout pairs one of each per explorer.

---

## Distance dial

### near — one step sideways
Adjacent industries, direct competitors, the tools used right before/after yours. Yields shippable, usable reshapes. These are idea-relative, so the Scout finds them via angles, not a fixed list:
- the killer feature of a direct competitor
- the tool your user opens right *before* or right *after* yours
- the same product moved across a line: consumer↔enterprise, B2B↔B2C, free↔luxury, solo↔team
- the offline / analog version of what you do
- you, rebuilt for a totally different audience (kids, pros, the elderly, another country)

### mid — another industry entirely
Unrelated sectors that solved a structurally similar problem. Seeds:
logistics & last-mile delivery · insurance & actuarial risk · hotels & hospitality · airline yield pricing · supermarket shelf layout · dating apps · personal training & fitness · public libraries · vocational training · real-estate listings · car dealerships · subscription boxes · event ticketing · loyalty programs · call-center scripting

### far — a different domain of reality
Nature, games, crime, art, sport, religion, science. Where the genuinely unexpected lives. Seeds:
slime-mold routing · ant-colony foraging · coral-reef symbiosis · casino & slot-machine psychology · speedrunning & route optimization · MMO guild economies · trading-card metagames · cartel logistics · prison economies & informal currency · street-magic misdirection · orchestral conducting · graffiti crews · monastic daily rituals · cult recruitment & retention · ER triage · special-forces small-unit tactics · jazz improvisation · competitive bodybuilding · auction houses · open-source maintainer culture

### absurd — break reality
Myth, dreams, children, aliens, the dead. For when you want to disorient. Seeds:
Greek-god domains & feuds · afterlife bureaucracy · dream logic & non-sequitur · a toddler's theory of cause and effect · alien first contact · haunted houses & poltergeists · time travel & paradox · fairy-tale curses & bargains · talking-animal fables · a heist crew · superhero secret identities · a board game nobody explained the rules to · purgatory waiting rooms · a wizard's apprenticeship

### wildcard — pure chaos
No theme. The Scout picks something with zero connection to the idea. The point is the non-sequitur — collisions no themed bucket would ever produce.

## Technique deck — how to transform once you've picked a domain

- **Inversion** — do the exact opposite of the obvious. Onboarding at the *end*. The app rewards you for *leaving*.
- **Subtraction** — remove the thing everyone assumes is essential. No accounts. No screen. No prices.
- **Exaggerate to extreme** — take one trait to 1000×. Absurdly slow / huge / public / expensive / private?
- **Biomimicry** — copy a living system's mechanism: how it routes, heals, reproduces, defends, signals.
- **Mashup** — fuse the idea with the domain literally: "X, but it's also a `<domain>`".
- **Constraint removal** — delete a constraint treated as fixed (cost, time, physics, law); see what opens up.
- **Forced constraint** — ADD a brutal limit: no internet / one button / 10 seconds / exactly one user, ever.
- **Persona transfer** — "how would a `<cartel boss / cathedral architect / toddler / casino host / cult leader>` design this?"
- **Time-shift** — how was this done in 1850? In 2200? What's the medieval / sci-fi version?
- **Role reversal** — flip who does the work: the customer becomes the seller, the audience becomes the show.
- **Status flip** — make the low-status part the headline; turn the boring backend into the product.
- **First principles** — discard the current form, restate only the core job, rebuild from the domain's logic.

## Wildness notes

- **grounded** — weight near/mid; keep ≥2 far/absurd lanes for spark. Break one assumption, stay shippable this quarter.
- **bold** (default) — full spread across all four buckets. Break core assumptions for real, keep a thread back to buildable.
- **unhinged** — weight far/absurd to ≥half the lanes; aim them at the most sacred cows; feasibility is information only, never a veto.
